Design References, Inspirations and Literature

In order to design this website I needed to do some research into other websites with aesthetics that were similar to the vision that I had for this website.

These websites were for both inspiration and reference as I made various design choices for the website.

I also took ideas and notes from the various readings given by the Interactive Media courswork. This literature helped to give insight into the process of website design, taking into account user experience and interface and various methodologies for web design.

I will be discussing all of these factors in the content of this page.

Design Inspiration/Reference #1

jessica-caldwell.com

A common factor I was looking for when researching various portfolio websites was minimalism, and so this website stood out particularily.

I like that there is an image of the person being represented by the portfolio, she is right there so that you know whose work you are perusing. This was a small inspiration between the drawings of my hands on the home page of my website. I wanted to give the user the impression that my hands are a representation of myself and what I use to create.

I like that the headings of the parapraphs are bold and large, even if the user does not have time to read the entire paragraph, the user can see at first glance what the designer wants you to know. This is the portfolio of a creative explorer, you can guage the meaning of the following paragraph from this.

The colour palette is very minimal, something I wanted to replicate in my own website. I like the small pops of colour that are added to this site, but I wanted to take my minimalist aesthetic a step further and only stick with black.

The navigation of the site is hidden as you are on the various pages, so that you can focus on the content of each page without looking at what else the website has to offer, allowing users to experience everything slowly and to take in all of the information. I really liked this part of this site.

This is a beautifully designed website with a wonderful minimalist aesthetic, it was very helpful for finding my own design style for this website. I am sure you can see some influence in how my bodies of text are structured.

Design Inspiration/Reference #2

kylethacker.com

Immediately, the minimalism of this site jumped out at me. It is extremely simple, but elegant. I can see the name of the person, in bold, and then what the person does.

There are not a lot of different elements on the page, a lack of images and such, this leaves the user to focus on the text, to take in the core, valuable information about this portfolio.

The colour palette of this site is also small, there are also pops of colour for some sub headings.

Much like the previous reference, this site was helpful in my design process for pages thick with text, such as the individual blog pages, the about page, and other design pages.

Design Inspiration/Reference #3

matthiashamman.work

At this point, you might be thinking that all of these sites are the same. Why am I showing all of them to you? I agree that there are vast similarities between all these references, but I think this is important, because I can pinpoint those very small differences between each site and decide what I like and what I do not like, all in a very small decision pool. It makes designing and making decisions a lot easier.

This site is minimalist like the two previous sites, but it has a much smaller colour palette. Exclusively black and white. It looks professional, it looks elegant. It could have colours, but I can see that colours would not add any more substance to the webpage, it stands on its own and looks beautiful.

I like how the name of the person is in a different font, a code related font, it makes it stand out and also tells the user that this person is in some kind of software development.

There is a lot of white space on this webpage, but it works to the advantage of the design. The users eyes can drift from each block of text with no other distractions. The white space shows that the user does not need to be overwhelmed by lots of information at once, the emptiness serves a good design purpose. I also wished to use emptiness in certain instances on my site.

Literature Review on Design

Metaphor Design for User Interfaces

This was an extremely interesting paper to read. The main point is that it is easier for people to understand user interfaces if they have a real life reference for them. For example: we understand how files and folders work in windows because we can relate these to real-life physical files and folders, we can discern their meaning in terms of the design of the windows file manager.

This paper inspired me to make a virtual, interactive art portfolio book for this website. Users can recognize that I am using the metaphor of a book to display my artworks. The user can turn through the pages, and navigate through the sections of artworks as if they are viewing them in a book.

I was very happy with the takeaway I took from this piece of literature, and with how I implemented what I learnt into my website.

Reference: Marcus. A,"Metaphor Design for User Interfaces",1998

A Crash Course in User Experience and Interface Design

This piece of literature takes the form of a powerpoint and a lecture given by Peta Goldberg.

The lecture was extremely insightful in terms of understanding the nuances between user experience and interface, and the design principles behind them.

The main takeaway from this lecture were the very basic pieces of information about website design. Readability. Thoughtful placement of elements. Thinking about the hierarchies of information. Another great part was using actual websites and social media applications to display these principles in action.

Overall, this lecture and its powerpoint helped me find my footing as I approached designing a website from scratch, and the powerpoint in question will definitely be something I look to for reference in the future.

Reference: Goldberg. P,"A Crash Course in User Experience and Interface Design",2022

Reflection on References, Inspirations, and the Literature

Compared to the previous version of this portfolio, I took more time with the references and literature in order to design this website, and I think that it paid off.

Without the websites referenced, I do not think I would have been able to find a pleasing style for this website, and I am very happy that I did manage to find one, at least in my opinion.

The two pieces of literature that I reviewed were amazing in helping me find a design process for this website. I did read other pieces given to us by the courswork, however I do consider these two pieces to be the most influential in terms of my work on this website.
