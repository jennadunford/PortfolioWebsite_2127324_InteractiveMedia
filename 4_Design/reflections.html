The Experience of Building a Website

So, websites are an extremely important part of life and society today. Websites are are avenues for communication, work, school, shopping, learning, discovering, sharing, publishing, entertainment, and probably a hundred thousand other things. And within all of these millions of websites and billions of webpages, there now exists a very own website of my own.

I by no means believe that I am an experienced web designer now, in fact I am far from it. But, I have learnt a lot about a very important part of the internet. When I visit websites now, I find myself contemplating how certain things were achieved, I even find myself checking out the html of the pages, the javascript, when I want to understand how something works. I have learnt how to build a website, and I think that my website looks pretty good.

The original focus for me was learning how to do things, but I have also discovered a new way to express myself, a new way to present myself online. I have experienced the ability to express myself through these lines of code, I have been able to make decisions and enact them at the very core of how a website works. What was my experience of building a website? It was a wonderful one. This design page, the style guide, has allowed me to reflect a lot on the all the small parts of this experience, and it has been fun, wonderful, and enlightening.

Reflections on Changes

On the subject of changes, referencing the website published for the previous assignment, the changes have been monumental. For this assignment, I really tried to delve into the deepness and importance of all the design elements that create a website, and I think that all that delving paid off. I am much, much happier with this website than I was with the previous one. The last website made me happy but it also felt like it was a dabble in web design at best, but this website feels like I succeeded in making a proper website, in a way. I am very pleased with it.

So, what changed? Everything. I changed absolutely everything. I started from scratch for this assignment. I changed the layout of the pages, I changed the navigation interface, I changed the colours, I changed how the art was displayed. I made these changes because I looked at the entirety of the first website and thought to myself: "I know I can do better." I actually had pretty much the same aims for this website as the first one, but I feel as if I have executed those aims effectively for this assignment.

Why do I feel I have executed the aims more effectively this time? I hope you have picked that up from all of the content in the design section of the website, I much more confidence in my reasonings, I have so much more to say in terms of my design. I believe that I can say with confidence that I have improved since the previous assignment, and I hope that I can continue to improve further.

A Personal View of my Webpage and my Growth as a Designer

Personally, reflecting on the design of the website, on everything I have discussed about the design, I feel as though this website represents myself as a designer and an artist. This website is a better representation of me than the previous one.

I think that I learnt more from the references I used for this website, I put a lot more thought into the layout of content in terms of the html, I learnt how to program navigation and my art portfolio using javascript.

I think that I have developed a better sense of user experience and interface and their design principles. I think I have managed to engage with my design of the UX and UI effectively.

And with that, I conclude the reflection section of the design guide of this website, and I also conclude the design guide of this website as a whole. Thank you for reading.
