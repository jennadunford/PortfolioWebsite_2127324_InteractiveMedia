I Am a Game Designer and Developer

My name is Jenna (she/her), I am a 21 year old Engineering and Digital Arts student at the University of the Witwatersrand, welcome to my About page.

What is there to know about me? I enjoy programming, I can code in Assembly, C#, C++ and Javascript. I have experience with making games in Unity, mainly 2D games.

I love to draw, I would love to someday pursue a career as a freelance illustrator. I love to explore the styles of various animated shows with my art, I love to use pastel and bright colours and I love to draw witches.

For all of the games I have made throughout my game design course, I have made my own art assets and have animated them as well.

My Creations and Experience

As part of my engineering degree, I had to progam a microprocesor using assembly code, while it was very basic, just programming an 8-bit LED light to display random numbers, making red and green lights flash with button presses, it was still challenging and very enjoyable.

I have made several games throughout my degree as well, including 2D and 3D games, I have experimented with platformers, turn-based combat games, physics-based games and exploration games.

You can exlore my abilites with regards to web design by taking a look at the website before you - which I made myself.

My Inspirations

My most important inspirations for my art comes from other artists. Akira Toriyama, the artist of Dragon Ball, is one of my favorites. I love his environment designs the most, beautiful pre-historic and futuristic environments creating these wonderful mystical worlds.

Another artist I admire is Julia Potts, creator of the cartoon show 'Summer Camp Island' - I love the whimsical character designs in the show, the cute faces that adorn trees, rocks and other environment elements. I love the colours of the show and the peace that it brings me.

As a game designer, I also look to games for inspirations. My favorite games include Stardew Valley, Animal Crossing: New Horizons, The Legend of Zelda: Breath of the Wild, and Don't Starve. I enjoy mainly cosy, peaceful games with beautiful art and gentle music. I enjoy Don't Starve for it's creepy Tim-Burton-Esqe art direction and for the thrill of the survival element.

For insight about my inspirations with regards to my website design, please take a look at the Inpirations section of the Design page.

Inspired by Akira Toriyama

Akira Toriyama is the artist and writer of the manga and anime that became a world-wide cultural phenomenon: Dragon Ball. There are few people in the world who have not at least seen a picture of the main character, Goku, as it is difficult to escape the vast reach of his story.

I started to watch Dragon Ball at the beginning of the covid-19 pandemic - in a time of confusion, fear, and solitude, it was difficult to immediately not fall in love with the characters, the artwork, the story, and the friendships that Toriyama had crafted.

Since discovering the amazing works of Akira Toriyama, I have kept them in mind as inspiration and reference for my own artworks and creations. I also take with me the messages from his stories - about friendship, love, courage, pride, and persevering despite every hurdle in the universe.

Inspired by @TSURUDRAWS

The internet and social media has been a goldmine of influences for art, I am so grateful that through the internet I have been able to discover some of the most amazing and wonderful artists and works of art.

One of my most absolute favorite artists that I discovered online was @tsurudraws on twitter. Their use of colour, their skill with character design, their linework, their painting, and everything else they excel at has deeply inspired and influenced my own art in wonderful ways.

Their artworks always bring me a deep sense of calm and happiness. There is something so gentle and soft in what they create. I strive in my own work to try and replicate these feelings, and they inspire me every day to improve my craft as I aspire to become an artist like them.

Inspired by ConcernedApe

Video games are a large part of my life and the things that bring me happiness. I love discovering amazing games that bring me endless joy and entertainment, especially games with which I can curl up under the covers on a rainy day and take in all their calm and gentle excitement.

Stardew Valley is one such game, and the dedication and love from its creator, Concerned Ape, truly comes through in every tiny detail and pixel in this charming farm-life simulator game.

It is always astonishing to find out that I game was created by one person alone - but the vastness of Stardew Valley really makes this an amazing feat. With this is the continued work and dedication that ConcernedApe continues to put into the game, although it came out years ago, it is far from abandoned with new updates and content being added, with the creator never stepping away from it.

If I manage to achieve my dream of someday being a sucessful game designer and developer in some capacity, I do hope to be like ConcernedApe, and I hope that whatever I made is even a fraction as wonderful as his games.

Inspired by Summer Camp Island

Animation is such a delightful medium. For animation to be effective, every frame needs to essentially be a work of art, every character's movements are meticulously thought out and choreographed by teams of extremely talented animators and artists. Even a minute of animation can equate to hundreds of hours of planning, drawing, and working.

All this makes animation itself a truly wonderful thing to experience, and one of my favorite animated shows is Summer Camp Island, created by Julia Potts.

When I first saw this show, I thought that it must have been made for me. The colours, the characters, the music, the humour - everything about it made me so happy and delighted. Summer Camp Island is such a wonderful show with especially wonderful art.

I love to spend time studying screenshots of the landscapes and foliage, learning about colour, composition, and design by trying to replicate the unique beauty of this show. Summer Camp Island inspires me to improve my art, and it is also a wonderful escape into a gentle and welcoming world.
